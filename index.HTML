Tadoba Tiger Reserves

The Tadoba Andhari Tiger Reserve is a wildlife sanctuary in Chandrapur district of Maharashtra state in India. It is Maharashtra's oldest and largest national park.


The Tadoba National Park is divided into three separate forest ranges, i.e. Tadoba north range, Kolsa south range, and Morhurli range , which is sandwiched in between the first two. There are two lakes and one river in the park, which gets filled every monsoon , the 'Tadoba Lake,' 'Kolsa Lake,' and 'Tadoba River'. Tadoba Reserve covers the Chimur Hill, and the Andhari sanctuary covers the Moharli and Kolsa ranges. Nearest village from this place is Durgapur. It is bounded on the northern and western sides by densely forested hills. Thick forests are relieved by smooth meadows and deep valleys as the terrain slopes from north to south. Cliffs, talus, and caves provide refuge for several animals. The two forested rectangles are formed of the Tadoba and Andhari ranges. The south part of the park is less hilly than the remainder.

Matkasur

Matkasur

MATKASUR is Tadoba's dominating male tiger. He is recognised as the current 'King of Tadoba' owing to his numerous victories over ancient 'Gabbar. ' Because of his enormous stature and 'pot-belly' physical structure, he was given the moniker 'Matkasur. ' Chota Matka, Choti Tara's cub, is supposed to be his father.

Matkasur, the famous tiger of Tadoba National Park, weighs over 250 kilograms and is considered a colossal tiger.

In his prime, Matkasur commanded control over some of the best areas inside Tadoba.

Matkasur and Maya were spotted mating in early 2017, giving birth to two cubs, Surya and Meera. A year earlier, Matkasur had mated with Chhoti Tara, also siring two cubs, Chhota Matka and Tarachand.

Choti Tara

Choti Tara

Choti Tara (T7) is a tigress in the Tadoba Andhari Tiger Reserve. She is known for her fighting spirit and determination, and her offspring rule the Alizanza zone and lake areas of Tadoba.

Choti Tara who is known for her bold nature. Born in the year 2009 she was raised by Yada-Anna and Tara. Choti Tara is named after her mother Tara. Her territory was overlapped with another bold and strong female tigress named Maya.

Choti Tara is known for her bold nature and has been in fierce fights with other tigresses and Choti Tara has raised six litters of cubs and is considered one of the most successful tigress mothers in the wild.

In 2024, Choti Tara gave birth to her seventh litter of two female cubs.

She is often seen in areas like Pandherpauni 1 and 2 which go up to Chikhalwai.

Bajrang

Bajrang

Bajrang from Tadoba was the stuff of a legend. Robustly built, with burly shoulders, forelimbs and a sinewy skull, Bajrang is the true exemplification of built-like-a-tank. Ironically, the name Bajrang is an epithet for the mythological Hindu god, Hanuman, and is derived from the word ‘vajra’ or the celestial weapon of Indra; which means as strong as the ‘vajra’ weapon.

In July 2014, Bajrang mated with Sonam,It was Bajrang’s first with any tigress, and that was the beginning of his chapter.

By the end of 2014, Bajrang captured Waghdoh’s favourite territory, the Waghdoh area, which incidentally became Bajrang’s favoured territory as well.

Bajrang had an indomitable reign, till the end of 2020, when he was challenged by a sudden influx of younger males. By then Bajrang had already sired 40+ cubs.

Chota Matka, the dominant tiger of Alizanja zone, got the best of Bajrang on 14th November 2023. A very badly mauled Bajrang seemed to have died instantly in the skirmish.

Maya

Maya

Maya (T-12) was the iconic tigress of Tadoba National Park, India, who was a popular attraction and a key factor in the park's fame.

Born in the Pandharpaoni area in December 2010, Maya’s lineage was rooted in Tadoba’s resilient tiger population.

Tigress Maya in Tadoba quickly gained worldwide attention, her image immortalised through thousands of lenses as she approached, often close enough for onlookers to hear the rustle of her footsteps through the underbrush.

Over her decade-long life, Maya, the tigress of Tadoba, raised five litters, producing 13 cubs in total.

Her maternal instincts were marked by an intelligence that enabled her to keep cubs alive even in the perilous high-density tiger territory of Tadoba With each litter, Maya’s reputation as a mother of exceptional resilience grew.

Sonam

Sonam

Sonam is a tigress from Tadoba Andhari Tiger Reserve. She is known for her fearlessness, maternal instincts, and regal presence. She is also the star of the documentary Telia Sisters.

Sonam is known for being fearless and dominant, and she defeated her sisters to become the leader of Telia.

Special Mentions

Waghdoh:
-Waghdoh tiger (T33) of Tadoba is the name of a famous male tiger that used to live in the Tadoba Andhari Tiger Reserve in Maharashtra, India.
-Waghdoh was known for its impressive size and strength and was very territorial and aggressive towards other male tigers.
-It was also known as 'Big Daddy of Tadoba' because of the marks on its face.
-It was the father of at least 40 cubs in Tadoba and the surrounding areas.

Waghdoh



Gabbar:
-Gabbar was a male tiger in Tadoba Andhari Tiger Reserve, Maharashtra, India.
-He was known for his large head, dark stripes, and a scar on his face.
-It was also known as 'Scarface' because of the marks on its face.

Gabbar



Chota Matka:
-There are many famous tigers in Tadoba. However, the most recently discussed tiger is called Chhota Matkasur by the local people.
-Chhota Matkasur was born in the core area to a tigress, called Chhota Tara, and a male, known as Matkasur .
-All the cubs in the buffer areas are fathered by Chota Matka, because of his size no other males have got a chance to dominate the buffer area.
-His warrior marks show how much he had to fight to defend his territory. He is the undefeated king of the buffer area and is considered to be the biggest tiger of Tadoba.

Chota Matka



Machli:
-Machali ,also known as Machli or Machhli, was a Bengal tigress who lived in Ranthambore National Park in India.
-India earned about US $10 million per year due to tourists attracted by the tigress.She won the "Lifetime Achievement Award" of Travel Operators For Tigers due to her contribution to conservation and as a tourist attraction that earned significant income for India.
-Machli had successfully hunted a crocodile, earning her the esteemed title of "Crocodile Killer".
-Machli is considered to have been the most photographed tigress in the world.
